The shortlist

15 names, strongest fit first

Order reflects strength of fit, not follower count. Read each card’s send angle before packing the pouch — the framing changes per recipient.

#1

Mary Ruddick

@maryqueenofheart · Instagram
🇺🇸 US ~28K Ancestral nutrition
What they do

Ancestral nutritionist studying traditional cultures’ diets in the field. “Sherlock Holmes of Health.” Speaker, podcast guest x100+.

Audience fit

Probably the cleanest pure-fit on the list. Studies actual ancestral food systems for a living. Audience is small but exactly Modern Savage’s ideological core. Female voice in a male-heavy category. Engages substantively with anything that survives her ingredient scrutiny.

Send angle “Real food not lab food” + “ancestral wisdom.” Note from Jesse on the formulation philosophy and a full ingredient/sourcing breakdown — she’ll read it cover to cover.
Risk flags

US-based — ancestral-nutrition space is global so reach still relevant. Smaller numerical reach but very high signal-to-noise.

#2

Ashleigh VanHouten

@themusclemaven · Instagram · Muscle Science for Women podcast
🇨🇦🇺🇸 Canada / US ~22K IG · 1.5M+ pod Ancestral nutrition
What they do

Author of It Takes Guts — one of the only nose-to-tail organ-meat cookbooks in print. Co-author of Carnivore-ish. Health coach, podcast host, mom.

Audience fit

Literally wrote the book on organ meats. Her audience is the warmest possible lead for an organ-inclusive whole-food scoop. Female voice in a male-heavy category. Podcast reach dwarfs her IG, so a podcast mention is the real prize.

Send angle “Real food not lab food” + nose-to-tail logic. Pouch + brand story + formulator’s note on organ inclusion. Floor for podcast mention.
Risk flags

Smaller IG reach. Possible existing relationships in the organ-supplement space (Ancestral Supplements, Heart & Soil) — verify no conflict before send.

@drcateshanahan · Instagram
🇺🇸 US ~55K Doctor-credible

Audience fit

MD credibility + ancestral framework + the seed-oil-free angle aligns perfectly with “Real food not lab food.” Her audience trusts her on what to put in their bodies — exactly the warm leads for a wholefood scoop. Female ancestral voice, no Liver-King taint.

Send angle “Proof not promises.” Lead with the ingredient deck — confirm zero seed oils, third-party testing, organ sourcing. Don’t market; let the formulation talk.
Risk flags

Has consulted for sports orgs (NBA Lakers, etc.) — not a serial product-endorser, so a post is uncertain. But if she likes it, the credibility unlock is huge.
