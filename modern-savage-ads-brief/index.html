Campaign Architecture

Every ad in this campaign is built from three layers: your voiceover narrating over stock footage and b-roll, with product shots of Modern Savage cut in at the bridge moments. No face-to-camera. No AI-generated clips. Just your voice, real footage, and the product.

Top of Funnel

Problem Aware

Make the audience feel the gap. Minimal or no product shown. Educate on the problems: supplement shelf chaos, synthetic ingredients, the gummy vitamin con. Pulls from Pillars 2, 3, and 4.

Objective: Video views / reach
Audience: Broad interests (health, family, clean eating, parenting)

Mid Funnel

Solution Aware

Introduce Modern Savage directly. The product, the family mission, the simplicity pitch. Product footage is prominent. Pulls from Pillars 1 and 2.

Objective: Traffic / engagement
Audience: Retarget TOF viewers + lookalikes

Bottom of Funnel

Trust + Convert

Bear's credibility, ingredient transparency, the formulation story. Permission to buy. Pulls from Pillar 5.

Objective: Conversions
Audience: Retarget MOF engagers + site visitors

How to Read the Scripts

HOOK First 1-3 seconds. Bold text on screen + a scroll-stopping visual. No VO yet.
YOUR VO What you record. Italic text = exact script. Grey text = delivery direction.
STOCK / B-ROLL Footage to source from stock libraries. Search terms included in green tags.
PRODUCT Modern Savage product footage you shoot yourself. Cut in where marked.
TEXT OVERLAY On-screen text. Animated or static depending on the moment.
CTA Closing frame with branding and call to action.

VO Recording Notes

Record in a quiet room. Phone voice memo works for testing; use a decent mic for finals. Natural conversational pace, not presenter-voice. Like explaining something to a friend over coffee. Warm, direct, grounded. Leave 1-2 second pauses between sections for clean edit points. Record each ad as one continuous take, then record each section individually as backup coverage.

Stock Footage Notes

Each ad includes green search-term tags for stock libraries (Pexels, Artgrid, Storyblocks, Envato Elements). Look for footage with natural light, warm tones, and a documentary feel. Avoid anything that looks like a pharmaceutical ad or a fitness commercial. Kitchen scenes, family mornings, natural environments, and close-ups of hands and objects will do most of the heavy lifting. Vertical (9:16) is ideal for Reels/Stories; source horizontal and crop if needed.

Product Footage You Need to Shoot

You can cover every ad in this brief with one product shoot. Here is the full shot list: the Modern Savage bag (Adult Blend) on a clean counter in natural light; the Kids Blend bag beside it; a scoop going into the powder; the powder being tipped into a glass; water being poured in and stirred; the mixed drink in a glass on a counter; both bags side by side; a close-up of the ingredient label; a close-up of the back-of-pack nutrition panel; the bag in a kitchen setting next to everyday items (kettle, fruit bowl, school bag). Shoot in natural morning light. Phone camera is fine if the light is good. Get both vertical and horizontal angles for every shot.

Meta Campaign Structure

How to organise these ads inside Meta Ads Manager. Three campaigns, mapped to funnel stage, with audiences and objectives.

Campaign 1: Awareness (Top of Funnel)

Objective: Video views (ThruPlay) or Reach

Ads: Ad 1 (Supplement Shelf), Ad 2 (Read the Label), Ad 3 (Gummy Vitamin Truth), Ad 4 (Running at 70%)

Ad Set A: Broad targeting. UK + US. Ages 25-45. Interests: clean eating, organic food, family health, parenting, whole foods, natural supplements, real food.

Ad Set B: Lookalike audience (once you have site traffic or email list). 1-3% lookalike from purchasers or email subscribers.

Budget split: 40-50% of total ad spend at launch. The job is to fill the retargeting pool. Test all four creatives; kill underperformers after 3-5 days based on ThruPlay rate and cost per ThruPlay.

Campaign 2: Consideration (Mid Funnel)

Objective: Traffic (landing page views) or Engagement

Ads: Ad 5 (One Scoop), Ad 6 (Family Mission), Ad 7 (For the Kids)

Ad Set A: Retarget: people who watched 50%+ of any TOF ad in the last 14 days.

Ad Set B: Retarget: people who engaged with the Modern Savage IG or FB page in the last 30 days.

Budget split: 25-35% of total spend. Measure on cost per landing page view and link CTR.

Campaign 3: Conversion (Bottom of Funnel)

Objective: Conversions (purchase or add to cart)

Ads: Ad 8 (Bear's Word), Ad 9 (What's in the Scoop carousel), Ad 10 (The Switch)

Ad Set A: Retarget: site visitors in the last 7 days who did not purchase.

Ad Set B: Retarget: people who watched 75%+ of any MOF ad.

Ad Set C: Retarget: add-to-cart abandoners (once pixel data allows).

Budget split: 20-30% of total spend. Measure on cost per purchase and ROAS. The carousel (Ad 9) will likely be your most efficient converter; the video ads (Ad 8, Ad 10) build trust that makes the carousel convert.

Testing and Iteration

Launch with all 10 creatives across the three campaigns. Let Meta's Advantage+ creative optimisation distribute spend toward the best performers. Review after 5-7 days. Kill any ad with a cost per ThruPlay (TOF) or cost per landing page view (MOF) more than 2x the campaign average. For BOF, give ads 7-10 days; conversion data needs time to attribute.

For the strongest-performing TOF ads, create 2-3 hook variations: different first 3 seconds, same body. The hook is the single biggest lever on Meta. Small changes in the opening frame can shift performance dramatically. You do not need to re-record the VO for these; just swap the opening stock clip and text overlay.

Once you have 50+ conversions, build a 1% lookalike from purchasers and layer it into Campaign 1 as a new ad set. This is how the funnel becomes self-feeding.

Priority Order (If Starting Lean)

If you want to launch with the minimum viable campaign and scale from there, start with these four ads. They cover the full funnel with the least production work:

TOF: Ad 1 (Supplement Shelf) + Ad 3 (Gummy Vitamin Truth). One targets the family-first adult, one targets the parent. Between them they cover the two biggest audience segments.

MOF: Ad 5 (One Scoop). The clearest product introduction. Uses the "replace the shelf" argument which is the strongest practical conversion driver.

BOF: Ad 9 (What's in the Scoop carousel). Cheapest to produce (static images), highest information density, and carousels consistently outperform video on cost per conversion at the bottom of funnel.
