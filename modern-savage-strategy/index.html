Social Media Strategy

Modern Savage

April 2026 · Pre-launch Strategy · UK + US

Prepared by Bear Witness

Executive Summary

Modern Savage is not a niche health brand for people who track macros. It is family nutrition done properly. Whole-animal organ blends in a single daily powder: everything your family needs in one scoop, with nothing your great-grandmother wouldn't recognise. This strategy builds the social presence to match that mission.

Every competitor in the organ nutrition space positions to the same audience: male, US, carnivore-adjacent. Modern Savage is the first brand in this category built deliberately for families. Not just a nod to a Kids SKU, but a whole-family mission from day one. That is the whitespace. That is how we win mainstream.

The strategy does not sell the product. It sells the problems the product solves. A parent frustrated with gummy vitamins enters through one door. A whole-food convert tired of managing eight different capsules enters through another. Both should feel like Modern Savage was built for them.

"Everything we build is in service of giving the body what it was designed to run on."

Seven mission narratives, distilled into five content pillars, deployed across a tiered platform ecosystem. The voice is warm but direct: it educates on what is missing from the modern diet and lets the product be the obvious solution. Bear Grylls is the proof, not the pitch.

Strategic Principles

Never sell the product; educate on the problems and let Modern Savage be the obvious answer. This is a family nutrition brand, not a biohacking brand, not a performance supplement, not a male health product. The audience is people who want to eat properly and have one simple way to do it. Bear is proof of concept, not a paid endorser. Every piece of content should pass one test: "Would someone thank us for this even if they never buy?"

Working Assumptions

This strategy assumes a clean-slate social launch (no existing accounts), Bear Grylls as primary on-camera talent with family/wellness UGC creators for audience breadth, and a lean-to-mid resource team at launch (~1-2 dedicated social operators plus freelance content creators). Adjust platform frequency and pillar weighting if resources change.

Mission Narratives

Seven distinct narratives. Seven doors into the brand. Each represents a real problem that a real audience segment feels. Three are primary, two are strong secondary, two are supporting. The product sits at the centre, but no single narrative dominates. The power is in the breadth: the more narratives a person encounters, the more inevitable the product becomes.

"Vitality is not a supplement category. It is what happens when you eat correctly."

Millions of people have accepted fatigue, brain fog, poor sleep, dull skin, and declining energy as normal parts of modern life. They are not normal. Organ meats contain CoQ10, B12, heme iron, retinol, and dozens of micronutrients in the forms the body evolved to absorb. The prebiotic and probiotic stack supports gut health, which underpins energy, immunity, and mental clarity. This territory takes the science seriously without being dry: accessible for a parent who is not a nutritionist but cares enough to learn.

Who Feels This Anyone who suspects they could feel better but has given up looking. Parents running on empty who assume tiredness is inevitable. Women navigating energy, hormonal shifts, and skin health. Men who have written off declining energy as aging. The person who "feels fine" but knows fine is not the same as good.
Transformation From: "I'm tired all the time but I think that's just how life is."
To: "I had no idea I could feel this different. This is how my body is supposed to work."
Emotional Intensity Revelation. The most powerful version of this narrative is the testimonial: the person who did not know what they were missing until they found it.

Tier 1: Launch Platforms Start Here

Instagram

Role: Brand world-building, education, community, and primary visual identity. Bear's personal account (8.3M followers) amplifies; the brand account builds its own following independently.

Key formats: Reels are the primary growth driver (family routines, ingredient education, Bear clips, "replace the shelf" content). Carousels are the authority builder (pillar-based educational breakdowns, "what's in your supplement" comparisons, one-scoop-vs-shelf visuals). Stories for daily connection and behind-the-scenes. Static posts anchor the grid with brand statements.

Frequency: 4-5 Reels per week, 2 carousels per week, daily Stories, 1-2 static anchor posts per week.

Critical note: Instagram SEO matters. Optimise captions and text overlays for search terms the audience uses: "whole food supplement," "kids nutrition without sugar," "natural family supplement," "organ nutrition," "replace supplement shelf." Instagram is now a search engine; treat it like one. Content should reflect the warmly grounded voice: inclusive, accessible, not niche-coded.

TikTok

Role: Discovery. Reaching new audiences at scale, especially the family and clean-eating audience (25-40).

Key formats: Short, raw, hook-driven clips (15-60 seconds). "What's on your shelf?" comparisons. Family morning routines. "Did you know" education from Pillars 2 and 3. Bear in the field. Customer reactions. UGC from family and wellness creators.

Frequency: 1-2 posts per day. Consistency matters more than production quality.

Critical note: TikTok rewards rawness. Nothing should look like an ad. The best content will feel like a friend telling you something they just discovered about their family's nutrition. Native captions, trending sounds with original angles. Family content and "replace the shelf" comparisons will be the strongest performers.

YouTube

Role: Deep authority. The evergreen library. Where people go when they want to understand, not just see.

Key formats: Long-form (8-20 min): "The Complete Guide to Family Organ Nutrition," "What's Actually in Your Kids' Vitamins," "Why We Built Modern Savage," "One Scoop: What's In It and What It Replaces." Shorts: repurposed TikTok/Reels clips.

Frequency: 1 long-form per fortnight (minimum), 3-5 Shorts per week.

Critical note: YouTube is a search engine. Title and thumbnail are everything. Build playlists around each pillar. Optimise descriptions with keywords: "best family supplement 2026," "are gummy vitamins good for kids," "whole food supplement vs multivitamin." Long-form builds the deepest trust and converts the highest-value customers.

Tier 2: Add When Stable Layer In

X / Twitter

Role: Brand voice, real-time commentary, industry opinion.

Key formats: Single-line conviction statements. Short threads on ingredient science. Quote tweets with a Modern Savage angle on family health topics.

Frequency: 1-3 posts per day once launched.

Substack

Role: Deepest narrative platform. Owned audience. Email list building. Where the mission lives in its fullest form.

Key formats: Weekly long-form essays rooted in each pillar. Deep-dives on organ nutrition science. Guest pieces from practitioners. Community discussion.

Frequency: 1 main post per week.

Critical note: Substack subscribers are the most valuable segment. The email list built through Substack is an owned asset that no algorithm can take away. This is the long-term moat.

General Capture Guidance

Prioritise authenticity over production value. Real environments, real families, real use. The audience this brand is built for can spot a staged photo instantly. Capture more than you think you need: one filming day should produce weeks of content if planned correctly. Always get vertical video for social (9:16) and horizontal for YouTube long-form (16:9). When possible, capture both simultaneously. UGC from family/wellness creators and early customers will be the most powerful content in the first 90 days. Make it easy for them: send product, send a brief, and let them create in their own style. Influencer seeding should lead with family and clean-eating creators, with ancestral health names as a secondary tier.
