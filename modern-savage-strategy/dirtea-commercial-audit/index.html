8. UNDER-TOLD STORIES

Based on this research, the following represent strong credentials that DIRTEA is NOT currently amplifying sufficiently on owned channels:

1. The Feeling Nuts Legacy (Pre-DIRTEA Social Impact)

Simon and Andrew created the Feeling Nuts Movement — a testicular cancer awareness campaign that became a prime-time Channel 4 show reaching almost 1 million live viewers, went globally viral, and led to their Forbes 30 Under 30 listing (2020). This demonstrates: - Proven ability to create cultural movements (not just products) - Health communication credentials before DIRTEA - Track record of making taboo health topics mainstream

Why it matters for ads: Positions founders as serial health communicators, not just supplement entrepreneurs. Almost entirely absent from current DIRTEA marketing.

Source: https://www.iangambier.com/feelingnuts ; https://wiki-gateway.eudic.net/wikipedia_en/The_Feeling_Nuts_Comedy_Night.html

2. Dr. Tara Swart's Post-Appointment Profile Explosion

Since joining as CSO (Oct 2024), Dr. Swart has appeared on Diary of a CEO (Aug 2025 — one of the UK's biggest podcasts), Jay Shetty, and published a NY Times/Sunday Times bestseller. Her public profile has grown enormously AFTER joining DIRTEA, but this credibility is barely being leveraged in brand content. She's one of the UK's most visible neuroscientists and is literally their CSO.

Why it matters for ads: "Our Chief Science Officer is a bestselling neuroscientist, MIT lecturer, and Diary of a CEO guest" is an extraordinarily strong proof point that's currently under-deployed.

3. The Bootstrap Growth Story (Revenue-Funded, Not VC-Backed)

DIRTEA has raised only ~$2M in funding yet achieved 230% CAGR and the #1 spot on UK's fastest-growing food & drink brands. In an era of VC-bloated DTC brands that collapse, DIRTEA appears to be genuinely profitable and revenue-funded. This is remarkable and differentiated.

Why it matters for ads: Authenticity signal. "We grew because people kept buying, not because investors kept funding" is a powerful counter-narrative to wellness brand scepticism.

4. The Celebrity Ecosystem (Organic, Not Paid)

The brand has an unusually long list of high-profile organic users (Rita Ora, Benedict Cumberbatch, Charlotte Tilbury, Idris Elba, Fearne Cotton, etc.) plus strategic investor-ambassadors (Bear Grylls, Roxie Nafousi). Most wellness brands either have celebrities OR science — DIRTEA has both, plus genuine cultural partnerships (Stella McCartney, Soho House).

Why it matters for ads: Social proof density is exceptional but fragmented across press mentions. Could be consolidated into a powerful "who trusts us" narrative.

5. Professional CEO Appointment (Institutional Maturity Signal)

Jessica Salmanpour was appointed CEO/Director in March 2024, with founders stepping into CCO (Simon) and CMO (Andrew) roles. This signals a business maturing beyond founder-led chaos into scaled operations — rare for a brand at this stage to publicly acknowledge.

Why it matters for ads: For B2B/retail pitches and investor credibility, this shows the business is run professionally, not just by charismatic founders. Under-communicated externally.
