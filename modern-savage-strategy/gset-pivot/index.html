GSET · Format Pivot

Immersive audiobook takes — the simple next steps.

Out: dramatised readings. In: 20 episodes of ~8-min immersive audiobook content, one professional reader, no new Bear footage. Here's what to actually do.

Prepared by Ted · 12 May 2026 · For Olly

The pivot, on one page

20 episodes total — one per chapter of the audiobook.

~160 mins of new filming, ~8 mins of usable footage per episode (8 min is the YouTube floor for mid-roll ads; aim 8:30–10:30).

One professional reader/performer across all 20 — single visual anchor for the series.

No new Bear footage — pre-recorded / archive only. Locked.

Everything else in the operator playbook (channel, Substack, calendar anchors, Give One Away) still stands. This doc only covers what changes because of the format pivot.

Decision needed first

What is the visual layer?

The audiobook audio carries narration (and possibly ambient/foley) for the full 8 minutes. So what is the camera actually capturing? Three plausible models — one of them needs picking on the next Bear call before any shoot is briefed.

ModelWhat you filmProsCons
A — Reader on-camera The performer silently / quietly performing each chapter to camera; audiobook plays over the top. Single visual anchor. Cheap. Recognisable face across the series. Reader has to be very good. Risk of feeling like a podcast-with-pictures.
B — Atmospheric / B-roll Visual representations of the chapter — landscapes, hands, candles, scripture text — no central performer. Genuinely immersive. Cinematic. Leans into the "footsteps in the audiobook" sound design. No human anchor. Edit-heavy. Higher cost.
C — HybridTed's pick Reader as the spine (A) + atmospheric B-roll cutaways (B) for ~30–40% of each episode. Anchor + immersion. YouTube-friendly. Best of both. Most expensive. Most editing.

Until this is answered, every other step downstream is guesswork. Worth 5 minutes of the next Bear call.

What changes vs. the existing playbook

  • On-camera cast collapses from five actors-as-disciples to one professional reader. Jesse's "five voices, one per disciple" line is overridden by the pivot.
  • Step 7 (clip factory) gets richer source material — every shoot day yields more footage than the episode uses; rest is clip fodder.
  • Q3 "first actor reading" milestone is replaced by first audiobook-take episode = 19 Jun anniversary drop.
  • Production model shifts from drama (casting director, multiple performers, costumes, scenes) to single-day(s) studio shoots with one reader. Significantly cheaper, significantly faster.
