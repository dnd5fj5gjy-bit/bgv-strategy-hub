The short version

One configuration that drives volume without cheapening the brand.

Tier 1 — a tightened customer referral programme that rewards loyalty, not bounty-hunting. Tier 2 — a small, named ambassador roster (6–8 creators, 10k–100k bracket) with a real commercial stack, not just "you're with Bear." Tier 3 — Jesse's angle, refined: 30 hand-picked creators receive a free pouch with zero post obligation, designed to generate earned mentions while protecting the premium feel.

None of it relies on Bear's ongoing content bandwidth. All three run in parallel from pre-launch through Q4 2026.

The Three Tiers

Each tier does a different job. Together they cover the channels we'd otherwise leave on the table.

Tier One

Customer Referral Promo

The compounding base

Existing-customers-only link. Referrer's next subscription month ships free when a referral converts. Referred customer gets 15% off first order — no deeper, because deeper cuts cheapen the brand.

Mode: Gated, share-your-shelf framing. No leaderboards, no MLM mechanics.
Tier Two

Named Ambassador Program

The narrative engine

6 ambassadors signed by July launch, growing to 8 by year-end. 10k–100k follower band. 15% recurring lifetime commission, free monthly product, quarterly brand-asset drops, soft competitor exclusivity.

Mode: Curated, invite-only. Scarcity is the premium signal.
Tier Three

Indirect Seeding (Jesse's angle)

The earned-media surface

30 creators in first batch, 20k–500k range, drawn from the existing 50-creator shortlist. Free pouch + formulator's note. Explicit "no obligation to post." The absence of an ask is the premium signal.

Mode: Hand-picked. Tracked via mentions, code redemptions, UGC pickup.
Tier One — Customer Referral Promo

The compounding base.

Mechanic

On-site referral hub gated by email capture. Customer enters name + email, gets a unique link and a 6-character code. When a new customer makes their first purchase via that link, the referrer's next subscription month ships free. The new customer gets 15% off — not deeper, because deeper cuts cheapen the brand and pull in the wrong buyer.

Why this is premium-safe

The reward goes to existing paying customers only. Framed as "share what's on your shelf," not "make money from your friends." No public leaderboards, no super-referrer status, no cash payouts. Rewards genuine word-of-mouth from people already using the product — how premium nutrition brands actually grow.

Promotion

Soft-launch in the order confirmation email and month-2 retention email. Single organic Meta post per month referencing the programme — never a paid ad. A line in the unboxing card: "If this works for your family, there's a way to share it."

Success markers. Month 1: 5% of customers generate a link, 1 in 8 of those drives a referral. Month 3: referred customers = 12–15% of new acquisition volume, retention at parity or better than paid-acquired customers.
Tier Two — Named Ambassador Program (10k–100k creators)

The narrative engine.

The honest read

"Be part of Bear's team" is a strong opener but not enough on its own to convert a working creator in this band. Creators here are running businesses — they need commercial terms that respect that. The Bear association gets the meeting; the stack closes it.

The stack we offer

  • 15% commission per sale, recurring on subscription renewals for the lifetime of the referred customer. Industry generic is 10% one-shot — we don't cap, and that's the differentiator.
  • Named ambassador status on a dedicated MS site page. 8 names max in year one. Scarcity is the premium signal.
  • Free monthly product (Adult Blend; Kids Blend on request for families).
  • Quarterly brand-asset drop — shoot-ready product photography, formulation cards, b-roll. Cheapest thing for us to provide; biggest unlock for working creators.
  • One Bear touchpoint at onboarding — signed welcome pack, batched, one-time. No ongoing Bear commitment.
  • Soft competitor exclusivity — cannot represent Heart & Soil, Lineage, Ancestral Supplements for the duration. Other categories fine.

What they commit in return

  • Minimum 1 organic post/month featuring MS — their format, their voice. We don't script.
  • Code sharing in bio for the duration.
  • One 15-min quarterly creative call.
  • Right of first refusal on paid usage of their content.
Target roster: 6 signed by 1 July 2026 launch, 8 by year-end. Small is the point.
Tier Three — Indirect Seeding (Jesse's angle, refined)

The earned-media surface.

Batch size + timing

30 creators in batch one, sent over a 6-week window beginning 6 weeks pre-launch. Drawn from the existing 50-name MS seeding shortlist. Second batch of 30 in October if batch one performs.

Follower bands

20k–500k (the existing MS seeding cap). Skew the batch toward the 30k–250k middle — under-30k lacks reach, over-500k rarely posts about a free send.

Selection criteria (all four must hold)

  • Genuine fit with one of the five MS content pillars — family, whole-food, real-ingredients, real life. No biohackers, no performance-supplement creators.
  • Documented history of posting about products they personally use, unprompted.
  • No active conflict (Heart & Soil, Ancestral Supplements, Lineage, or equivalent).
  • UK/US weighted 60/40 to match the launch geography.

Seeding package contents

  • One Adult Blend pouch (Kids Blend added for families).
  • A printed one-page formulator's note from Jesse — his voice, why it exists, sourcing one-liners, allergen call-out.
  • An ingredient-sourcing card.
  • A handwritten note — no PR-agency template.
  • A discrete personalised code with 15% off — matching the customer-referral discount, never deeper.

The ask, in writing

"We made this for families like yours. We'd genuinely love to know what you think. There is no obligation to post — if you do, that's a bonus, and your code is in the pack." The lack of ask is the premium signal.

What we track

  • Mentions (existing social-listening tooling).
  • Code redemptions per creator (signal, not target).
  • UGC pickup — request rights, re-share, or run as paid.
  • Reply rate to the seed (proxy for warmth, leading indicator).
How the three tiers combine

Each tier covers what the others can't.

Tier 1 — Compounding base

Every happy customer becomes a low-cost acquisition channel. Without it we leave the cheapest channel on the table.

Tier 2 — Consistent narrative

6–8 trusted voices showing MS in their lives every month. Named, on-record advocates. Gives paid social fresh creative.

Tier 3 — Earned reach

Bigger, less predictable mentions with no commercial tie — which is what makes them credible. Without this, the programme reads as "another supplement affiliate stack."

Premium guardrails

The lines that hold the brand at premium even while volume grows.

No discount deeper than 15% to anyone, ever — referrer, creator, ambassador audience.
No public leaderboards, no top-earner badges, no MLM-feel mechanics.
No paid-post obligations in Tier 3 — the absence of an ask is the entire point.
No mass affiliate sign-up form. Tier 2 is curated, invite-only.
No stacked discount-code spam in creator bios. MS code only, or they're not the right ambassador.
No celebrity bounty offers. The brand pays in product, equity-of-association, and recurring commission. Not cash bungs.
Bear appears in the onboarding pack and ambassador-page footer only. Never in a scripted creator caption.
