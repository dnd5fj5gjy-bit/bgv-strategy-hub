01 · Where we are

A bestseller, a strong publisher framework, and a decision to run it ourselves.

The Greatest Story Ever Told by Bear Grylls launched in June 2025 and hit #1 Sunday Times — the first Christian title in Hodder Faith's history to do so. 4.8★ across 1,400+ Amazon reviews. 288 pages. A narrative built around five eyewitness voices.

In March 2026, Hodder delivered a thoughtful 9-section framework for a publisher-driven, community-owned promotion engine that didn't depend on Bear's time. Hodder hasn't actioned it. BGV is going to. The shift from "publisher promoting a Hodder asset" to "BGV owning the channel and monetising the audience" changes what we keep, what we layer in, and where the moat is. This document is the plan for that. From here on, "GSET" is shorthand for the full title.

"Never sell the product. Educate people on the problems it solves." — The marketing philosophy from the Hodder framework
One non-negotiable

Bear's bandwidth constraint stays. The Hodder doc was right about this — no new filming, no new podcast or audiobook recording, only 2–3 calendar-anchored posts/year on his channels (written by the BGV team and posted on his behalf), and occasional Story reposts at his discretion. Bear's time is genuinely scarce across BGV's portfolio — TV, expeditions, family, the wider brand network. Anything in this plan is built to run without new Bear content. If execution starts depending on it, the plan is breaking.

The Hodder framework's central marketing principle: don't market the book, address the audience problem the book answers. Each door defines a content lane and a viewer state-of-mind.

03 · The five voices

The book's structure is its content engine. Five eyewitness perspectives — five readers, five lanes.

The narrative spine of The Greatest Story Ever Told is five first-person testimonies. That's not just literary — it's our casting brief, our calendar, and our format. Each voice maps to a different audience door and gives us five distinct emotional registers to work with.

i.

Maryam (Mary, mother of Jesus)

The watching mother. The longest arc — birth to cross. Tone: tender, devotional, family-facing.

ii.

Ta'om (Thomas)

The doubter. Honest scepticism, then encounter. Tone: questioning — perfect for the Curious Outsider.

iii.

Shimon (Simon Peter)

The action lead. Failure, restoration, calling. Tone: gruff, redemptive — the soldier-of-faith arc.

iv.

Yohannan (John)

The beloved disciple. Closest to the inner circle. Tone: poetic, contemplative — emotional peak chapters.

v.

Maryam of Magdala (Mary Magdalene)

The first witness of the resurrection. Tone: raw, redemptive, female-led — Easter centrepiece.

04 · The strategic shift

What survives from Hodder. What BGV adds. What changes when we own it.

Hodder's framework is good. The Bear-bandwidth constraint inside it is real — not a publisher concession. BGV's edge is not "more Bear time." It comes from owning the engine: editorial control and speed, an owned audience, commerce attribution into the BGV brand network, and faster non-Bear commissioning. The plan is built to deliver on those without asking Bear for anything new.

What survives

  • The five-voice principleDifferent actors per eyewitness — emotional range and casting variety.
  • Twin-engine YouTube + SubstackPublic library + private study. Still the right two platforms.
  • Calendar anchoringLent, Holy Week, Pentecost, Anniversary, Autumn, Advent.
  • The Reader Journey loopDiscover → Engage → Relate → Act → Give. Loop, not funnel.
  • The five doorsEducate on the problem. Never advertise the product.
  • The Bear-bandwidth constraintNo new filming, no new podcast or audiobook recording. 2–3 calendar-anchored posts/year on his channels, written by BGV. Occasional Story reposts at his discretion. That's it.

What BGV adds

  • An on-screen host (Olly)Olly fronts the channel under the GSET banner. Bear is patron, not host. No new Bear time-cost.
  • Editorial control + speedBGV commissions and ships without publisher sign-off cycles. Hodder is a partner, not a gatekeeper.
  • Owned-audience moatSubstack list and YouTube subscribers belong to BGV. They convert across the BGV portfolio over time.
  • Commerce attributionBGV can route faith-aligned audience into adjacent BGV products where genuinely brand-aligned — a publisher cannot.
  • BGV brand-network cross-promoModern Savage, Be Military Fit, Ted's Health share a values-driven, family-first audience overlap. Where it's native, share reach.
  • Faster commissioning of non-Bear contentTheologian conversations, actor readings, reader-spotlight pieces, host-led discussions. We move at BGV pace, not a publishing schedule.

What changes

  • BGV preserves Hodder's bandwidth constraint on Bear.The shift is in editorial control, owned audience, commerce attribution, and faster non-Bear commissioning — not in asking Bear for more time.
  • Substack is now a moat, not a tacticHodder sold books. We're building an owned audience for life.
  • Audio rights are a pre-conditionThe clip factory dies without recut rights. Resolve in week one.
  • The Chosen crossover gets aggressiveHodder mentioned it; for BGV it's the single biggest external unlock.
  • De-prioritise low-ROI lanesPrisons / military / schools — great PR, slow commerce. Keep, but late.
  • Need a sales-data agreementIf BGV is funding the engine, BGV needs the conversion read.

06 · The twin-engine thesis

YouTube and Substack are the two platforms. Everything else feeds them.

Hodder's framing — and one we keep. YouTube is the public library: searchable, evergreen, algorithmic discovery. Substack is the private study: owned email, weekly cadence, intimate. They feed each other. Every YouTube video invites to Substack; every Substack links to YouTube.

07 · The reader journey

A loop, not a funnel. The reader's story becomes someone else's discovery.

The Hodder framework's most useful structural insight: this isn't a one-shot conversion. A reader who acts becomes a reader who gives, and that gift is someone else's discovery. The whole system is designed to close the loop.

01

Discover

TikTok, Reels, YouTube search. The audiobook clip factory does the work here.

02

Engage

YouTube channel, Instagram. The five-voice readings, the Olly-hosted conversations.

03

Relate

Substack, Facebook / Discord, church partnerships. Where shallow becomes deep.

04

Act

Website buy, church sign-up, Big Church Read. The conversion moment.

05

Give

Gifting, sharing, reader-story submission. The act that re-seeds the loop.

The loop closes here. A reader who gives the book becomes someone else's "Discover" moment — and the cycle restarts.

08 · Calendar anchors

The Christian liturgical calendar is the publishing schedule.

Six anchor points define the year. Off-peak is steady cadence (3–5 posts/week — testimonials, quote graphics, audiobook clips, community engagement). At anchor points, we surge.

Lent From 18 Feb 2026
The 5-part book maps cleanly to the 5 weeks of Lent. Activate the Big Church Read with Lent guides. Substack runs a 5-week series. YouTube: crucifixion / resurrection clips and Maryam-of-Magdala-led readings.
Holy Week / Easter 29 Mar – 5 Apr 2026
Emotional peak. The most shareable week of the year. Yohannan and Maryam of Magdala lead the content schedule. Daily clips. Calendar-anchored Easter post drafted by BGV and posted on Bear's channels (one of the 2–3 a year).
Pentecost 24 May 2026
The book's final scenes — Ascension and Holy Spirit. Frame: "The story doesn't end at the resurrection." Shimon-led content. No Bear ask — channel content carries this anchor.
One-year anniversary 19 Jun 2026
UK publication anniversary. Reader-story roundup. The "year-on-year" testimonials moment. Substack longform retrospective. YouTube: best-of compilation + new actor reading.
Autumn church push Sep – Oct 2026
Denominations are planning their winter programmes. Activate the church partnership pipeline. New Big Church Read cohorts. Substack pivot to "small group" tone.
Advent / Christmas From 29 Nov 2026
The biggest sales window of the year. Full Give One Away campaign in market. Daily Advent content. Church bulk orders. Calendar-anchored Christmas post drafted by BGV and posted on Bear's channels (the second of the 2–3 a year). Maryam (mother) leads.

Without recut rights to the audiobook, the entire clip factory and ~30% of the YouTube engine doesn't run. Resolve in Week 1, before any spend.

High · Wk 1

Bear bandwidth — the real constraint

The plan is built to respect the existing Hodder constraint: no new filming, no new podcast appearances, only 2–3 calendar-anchored posts/year on Bear's channels. If anything in execution starts depending on new Bear content, the plan is breaking and needs a rethink. Audit every content brief against this rule before commissioning.

Medium

Hodder data sharing

Medium

14 · Open questions

Four questions to put to Hodder and Bear before we commit.

?

Audio rights. Does BGV already hold recut rights to the Bear-narrated audiobook, or do we negotiate? This is the gating question — the engine depends on the answer.

?

Editorial structure. Freelance editor or in-house? This plan assumes freelance for cost flexibility — confirm before hiring.

?

Confirm patron role. Is Bear comfortable with Olly hosting the channel under the GSET banner — assumes no new content asks, his role is patron + occasional Story repost at his discretion? This is a confirmation conversation, not a negotiation of new deliverables.

?

Revenue share with Hodder. If BGV's engine drives book sales, what's the counterparty position? Referral fee, affiliate, royalty share on YouTube monetisation? Worth raising while the rights conversation is open.
